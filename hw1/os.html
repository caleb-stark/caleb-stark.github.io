What an OS does

An operating system is the main software that manages hardware and runs applications. It controls memory, files, and access to devices like keyboards and network adapters. Without an OS, most computers would not be usable for everyday work.

Examples include Windows, macOS, and Linux. Different systems focus on different goals, like ease of use, flexibility, or compatibility with certain programs.
